Why the long gap? I got a new pair of cans. And they are pretty mind blowing as well. So I spent a lot of time re listening to a lot of songs through these godlike headphones. I am talking about the [Audio Technica ATH-M50](http://www.flipkart.com/audio-technica-ath-m50-wired-headphones/p/itmd6hk8b9ff2rhh?pid=ACCD6HK7FFJRFSAP&start=8999&otracker=nf_price_cart), which is a proven performer among the plethora of headphones in the market. Although failed to get it at around 5.5K Rs from amazon.com, got it finally from Flipkart@9k. The headphones seem to have lived through its burn-in time and sounds amazing. If you are willing to spend some money on a quality pair of cans, this one is a must buy.

Despite all this, I listened to lots of new stuff during this period. Beginning with Skyharbor, they released another single from their upcoming *Guiding Lights*. The song, titled *Patience*, is a move towards a softer side away from *Evolution*, which was there in my Ultimate June playlist. The video is deeply moving, so is the whole song. Expecting great things from the upcoming album.

I also tried a little Gojira's *From Mars to Sirius*, which exactly gives me the kind of heaviness I love breaking my neck to. Left rest of it for future exploring, knowing I will like it anyway. A little Porcupine Tree was also found being played on my awesome headphones during early August. Also never having tried Slayer, I tried couple of iterations of *Reign in Blood*, which although I liked it, felt like it needs more careful listen from my side. Tried (and loved) couple of songs from  Opeth's *Pale Communion*.

The overall winner since last ultimate playlist is my more recent venture into **At the Gates**. It lies somewhere between death metal and melodeath. The album, *Slaughter of the Soul* is a killer, and it was a love at first listen for me.

To satisfy my OST addiction, I spent a good amount of time listening to the Final Fantasy Type-0 OST (which btw awesome!, both the game and OST).

Time for some guilty pleasure. I sneakingly got hooked to few Within Temptation ballads for quite a while. Although I hate Within Temptation for transitioning to rock away from style in *Mother Earth*, I still sometimes, when nobody (the elitist me) is looking, indulge myself in it.

Plans for November? Apart from the new releases from Skyharbor and Pink Floyd (!), more Death!
