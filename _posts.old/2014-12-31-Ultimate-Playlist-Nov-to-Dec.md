When I listened to *The Sound of Perseverence* for the first time few months back, I realized the bars of high quality death metal. I knew for sure that it will be extremely difficult to come across an album of such virtuosity and depth. But a couple of months later, an album from the same band blew me away. *Symbolic* made me realize why *Chuck* is called The Father of Death Metal, and **Death**, the most essential Death Metal band. *Symbolic* was too perfect, with each and every song a violent blast of emotions and power. I said, "Okay, Death got two legendary album to its name, I bet the rest wouldn't be close to these". How wrong I was. *Spiritual Healing* left me speechless. I have been banging my head to it constantly during the past couple of months. It is difficult to have a favourite song in *Spiritual Healing*, just like it is impossible to have a favourite **Death** album. There is no best **Death** album, the band itself is godly. Period. No other band except **Iron Maiden** has had the immense respect and reverence from me, and **Death** is the new entry (A bit late, but I am still a blooming metalhead). That being said, *Spiritual Healing* would be the star of this article's playlist (Though it has been one of the most difficult tasks of shortlisting songs from this album for the playlist).

I dived into some ambient post-rock stuff from **Tide's from Nebula**. Each track puts you on a feel train to the kingdom of emotions. There is a uniqueness in their tracks, with beautiful and stable song structures.

Also have been trying **Arch Enemy**, and am not disappointed. It sounds pretty much like stereotypical melodic death metal, but done really well. I can probably enjoy it for a couple of months. From the few songs I tried, I seems they decide on a very catchy riff and part of lyric, and build a song that heavily relies on both these elements.

Other entries include assorted tracks from various other artists. Few tracks from **Judas Priest**'s' *Redeemer of Souls*, **Skyharbor**, **Pink Floyd**'s latest *The Endless River* and **Mastodon**'s *Blood Mountain* got hold of a position in my music routine.

I guess I will finish this year with this playlist. :)

Plans for the New Year's first month? Went through the first pass of **Death**'s *Human*, was great as expected, will be giving more listens. **Mastodon**'s *Blood Mountain* will also get more attention. Will try more of **Tides from Nebula**, **Arch Enemy**, Also, getting hold of **Skyharbor**'s new *Guiding Lights* would be a mission (Amazon region restrictions plus nowhere else to find in India :(  ).
