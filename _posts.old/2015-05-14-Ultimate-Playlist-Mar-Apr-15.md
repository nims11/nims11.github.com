These couple of months have been very Opethy. Very rarely I come across something so complex yet perfect, and so intense yet beautiful, The songs gets into your mind and syncs itself well with your emotions, and once that happens, you will feel the intensity behind each and every riffs, beats and vocals.

I started off with *Ghost Reveries*. Ghost of Perdition was the first Opeth song I had ever listened to, so I had high hopes from this album. And brilliant it was. There was so much awesomeness crammed into the songs that I often found myself rewinding them in order to absorb it till satisfaction.

Next was *Damnation*. It is softer than the other Opeth works, and certainly not anything close to heavy or brutal. Nonetheless, *Damnation* is certainly a golden work. Songs are slower but extremely well written and executed. The music is deep enough to get you into the feel train.

Currently in middle of trying *Blackwater Park*. I had already heard few of the songs from this album including the famous Blackwater Park, Drapery Falls, Harvest, etc. So it is quite easy to like it. However, will include it on the next post.

I tried nothing major except Opeth during these two months due to various reasons and exams. Have a lot of time in hand in the coming weeks, will try more Opeth, more Death, and who knows what.

### Playlist
- Ghost of Perdition (Opeth)
- The Baying of the Hounds(Opeth)
- Beneath The Mire (Opeth)
- Reverie/Harlequin Forest (Opeth)
- Windowpane (Opeth)
- In My Time of Need(Opeth)
- Death Whispered a Lullaby(Opeth)
- Closure (Opeth)
- Hope Leaves (Opeth)
- To Rid the Disease (Opeth)
- Sign of The Cross (Iron Maiden)
