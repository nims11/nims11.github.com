title: Game Time - To the Moon

Steam is doing a great job bringing games to Linux, making them easily accessible to the users. As a result, I did my fair bit of shopping in the *too good to be true* steam end of the year sales. One of the purchase was *To the Moon*.

More than a game, it is an interactive story, 16-bit JRPG style. The concept itself was intriguing enough, let alone the overwhelmingly positive user reviews. JRPG style, good music, and story are my thing, so I had to get this. One fine night, I thought to starting off this game, and the next thing I knew, It was five in the morning, with the ending credits rolling, mind blown, eyes wet, and a strange but nice emotion flowing through.

The story follows two doctors, who set out to fulfill the wish of a dying man, by using a technology which allows them to traverse through the patient's memories. The fulfilled wish exists only in the memories of the patient, so he can die happily. The memory traversal device require the doctors to traverse through the memories of the patient in a reverse timeline. So, as the story proceeds, you get to know more about the characters, with various fragmented pieces eventually coming together. That being said, the story is the strongest point of the game, given there is not a lot of gameplay involved except for moving around and interacting with objects. With a concept like this, there is a lot of scope of flaw picking. But I failed to get any major ones even after my second playthrough. There is a lot of good humour and emotion in the story and direction. *To the Moon* confirmed my belief that complex stories can be showcased brilliantly through old school graphics.

The characters have a strong development throughout the story. As we move through the story, we come to know more about the younger selves of the characters and the reasons which drived their actions we witnessed earlier. The story weaves them together beautifully. And the fact that most of the characters are just a part of the patients memory, is quite something. The doctors, too have an essential presence, with *Neil Watts* serving as the much needed comical character in the game.

Last but not the least, the Music. The music fits *SOO* well with the entire game. I spent 5 hours playing the game, another 5 replaying it, and 10 hours listening to the OST. If not for the music, I would have still liked the game, but not enough to write this post about it. The music pieces just drop in at the right moment, along with the rightly timed sound effects which just goes along with the mood.

> 9/10, specially for the price I got this game at. Desperately waiting for the Episode 2 (There are few disturbing questions which left unanswered!).
